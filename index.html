CitiBike users in New York are on the move, performing roughly 800,000 more trips in Sept. 2021 from Sept. 2020 â€” a roughly 32 percent increase â€” according to Citibike data.

Between Sept. 2020 and 2021, roughly 400 new stations came online, which helped aid ridership in northern Manhattan and the Bronx. By Sept. 2021, that number dropped to 1,136, a 22 percent decrease.

Where New Yorkers began their rides

Despite the new stations, Citibike usage remained centered in lower Manhattan, which some of the top-five most popular stations for beginning and ending rides staying the same across both months.

Sept. 2020

  1. West 21 St & 6 Ave: 13,875 rides started

Sept. 2021

  5. W 20 St & 10 Ave: 12,918 rides started

While it seems like Citibike users are still starting their trips in lower Manhattan, data shows they are more likely to end their trips across the five boroughs, when comparing stations where trips were completed between Sept. 2020 and 2021.

Where New Yorkers finished their rides

The median number of trips ended per station also dropped drastically, from roughly 1,435 trips completed per station to 431, a nearly 70 percent decrease.

Citibike data shows an increase of over 50 percent in stations used to end trips between the two months, which could account for such a drastic decrease in median trips ended per station.
